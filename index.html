  • How does an ETF differ from an index mutual fund?

    An ETF (Exchange Traded Fund) is an investment product that represents a basket of securities such as the S&P 500 Index. Unlike mutual funds, which are priced once a day after the US market closes, ETF prices change throughout the day because they're traded like shares.

  • What are the advantages of ETFs versus mutual funds?

    ETFs typically charge 75% lower fees than actively managed mutual funds and offer investors a wide range of sectors, geographies and strategies.
